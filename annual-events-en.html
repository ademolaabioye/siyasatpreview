    Track One: Track Title

    Paper in English Previous Title

    Why was Turkey's bloodiest coup the most failed of all coups?

    (Published version)
    Download paper

    Subtitle

    Despite the availability of many factors for the coup's success, such as the element of surprise (at least in terms of timing), weak international reactions, and the rebels' possession of significant firepower such as the aircraft and tanks that took to the streets of Istanbul and Ankara amid the participation of various branches of the armed forces and senior officers in the Turkish army, the coup attempt failed. The most prominent reason for its failure was the consensus of the political class, intellectual elites, the media, and broad segments of the Turkish people in rejecting the coup.

    Hamza Ibrahim Muhaisen
    Researcher in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

    Researcher in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.An optional extra line.
